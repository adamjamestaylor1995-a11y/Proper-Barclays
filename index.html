How to Play
The Basics
Build a 7-player squad from the Barclays Premier League era (2004–2014) and compete through cup rounds and league seasons. Each player has a budget, so spend it wisely across your goalkeeper, defenders, midfielders and attackers.
The Draft
Players are organised into three tiers: Budget (under £5m), Mid Range (£5m–£10m) and Premium (£15m+). Pick one slot at a time. Run out of money and you'll be limited to free agents, so don't blow it all up front.
Manager
You'll be offered three managers, each with a unique bonus effect. Some boost specific nationalities, others reward a certain formation or playing style. Choose wisely. A well-matched manager can make a real difference over a long season.
Formation and Style
Choose from four formations (2-2-2, 2-3-1, 3-2-1, 3-1-2) and three build-up styles (Short, Direct, Hybrid). These affect how your team creates and concedes chances in each match.
Matches
Each match plays out as a series of attacking phases: passes, dribbles, aerial duels, and shots. Player stats determine the outcome of each phase. Watch it live or simulate instantly.
Leagues and Cup
You start in League D and can win promotion all the way to League A. Each season also features a Domestic Cup with knockout rounds. Win trophies to earn prize money and improve your squad in the Transfer Market between seasons.
Squad Builder Bonuses
Certain squad combinations unlock hidden bonuses, such as seven different nationalities, all players from one club, or specific player pairings. Check the squad summary screens to see which bonuses you've activated.
The End Goal
Your ultimate aim is to become the most decorated club in England. Win league titles and cups across multiple seasons to climb the all-time trophy table. The game ends the moment your club reaches the top of that table. Check your progress at any time via All Time Records on the post-season screen.
Pre-Season
Between seasons you enter a Pre-Season screen before the next campaign begins. Here you can sell players via the Transfer Market and buy replacements. You can also change your formation, but only after selling at least one player first. If you have the budget, you can sack your manager and pick a new one with a fresh bonus. Changes to formation affect which players fit where, so plan your transfers and formation together.

Make use of the pre-season window to change your playing staff, manager, formation, and style of play ahead of the next campaign.
